ADHD Women's Health App
Product Plan & Build Roadmap
Version 1.0  |  March 2026
What this document covers
This plan defines the product vision, MVP scope, content architecture, tech stack options, monetization model, and immediate next steps for building a research-backed women's health intelligence app designed for ADHD minds.


1. Product Vision
A structured, ADHD-accessible health intelligence platform where women understand their body patterns, make informed decisions, and track health changes over time. This is not a blog. It is a research-backed, organized knowledge system.

Core Promise
	•	Evidence-based women's health content — organized, not overwhelming
	•	Built for ADHD brains: short, visual, action-oriented
	•	Pattern recognition: connect symptoms, hormones, cycle, and lifestyle
	•	Not diagnostic — educational and empowering

Target User
Who
Women ages 25–45 with ADHD traits or executive dysfunction
Pain Point
Overwhelmed by fragmented, dense medical information — no clear starting point
Desire
To understand their own body, find patterns, and feel less alone
Device
iOS mobile — daily use, habit building, on-the-go access


2. MVP Scope — What You're Building First
MVP Focus (agreed)
Content Library + Symptom Tracker. The goal is to deliver enough value that users open the app daily and begin to see their own health patterns.

2.1 What's In the MVP
Feature 1: Content Library
	•	Topics organized by health area (not alphabetically — by relevance)
	•	Each topic contains a structured 'knowledge card':
	•	Quick Summary: 3–5 bullets, plain language
	•	ADHD Connection: how this topic affects ADHD symptoms
	•	Pattern Spotters: what to track and watch for
	•	Evidence Rating: labeled by strength (Research-backed / Expert consensus / Emerging)
	•	Western vs. Functional vs. Eastern Medicine view (where applicable)
	•	Action Steps: 2–3 concrete things to do or ask your doctor

	•	Topic list for MVP launch:
	•	Hormonal cycles & ADHD
	•	PMDD
	•	PCOS
	•	Perimenopause & executive dysfunction
	•	Thyroid & cognition
	•	Mental health & ADHD overlap
	•	Stress & inflammation

Feature 2: Symptom Tracker
	•	Daily check-in (2–3 min max — ADHD-friendly brevity)
	•	Track daily: mood, energy, focus, cycle day, sleep hours, key symptoms
	•	Pattern view: weekly chart showing correlations (e.g., focus dips at cycle day 21–28)
	•	Export: simple summary for doctor appointments

Feature 3: Onboarding & Profile
	•	3-screen onboarding: select conditions/interests, set tracking preferences
	•	Personalized content feed based on selected conditions
	•	Reminder setting for daily check-in

4. Tech Stack Options
You're starting from zero without a developer team. Here are three realistic paths, ordered by build complexity.

Option A: No-Code First (Recommended Starting Point)
Best for: Validating your concept before spending on development. Fastest to get real user feedback.
Use Glide Apps or Bubble.io to build a working prototype in weeks, not months.

	•	Tools: Glide Apps (mobile-first, no code) or Bubble.io (web, no code)
	•	Timeline: 4–8 weeks to working prototype
	•	Cost: $100–500/month subscription, no developer needed
	•	Limitation: Not a native iOS app — web-based or limited native wrapper
	•	Use this to: test content structure, validate daily tracking habit, get first 100 users

Option B: React Native + Expo (Recommended for iOS Build)
Best for: Building the real iOS app. Cross-platform (iOS today, Android later). Large developer community.
You'll need to hire a developer — this is the path to the App Store.

	•	Tech: React Native + Expo + Supabase (backend) + Expo Router (navigation)
	•	Timeline: 3–5 months for MVP with 1 developer
	•	Cost: $15,000–$40,000 with a freelance/contract developer
	•	App Store ready: Yes — submits to Apple App Store
	•	Future-proof: Add Android later with same codebase

Option C: Native SwiftUI (Premium iOS Only)
	•	Tech: SwiftUI + Supabase or Firebase
	•	Timeline: 4–6 months with a Swift developer
	•	Cost: $25,000–$60,000+
	•	Best performance: Yes — but iOS only forever
	•	Harder to find developers: Swift specialists cost more

Recommendation
Start with Option A (no-code prototype) to validate your content and user behavior. Once you have 50–100 active users and proof of concept, move to Option B (React Native) for the real iOS app. This reduces risk and cost significantly.

5. Content Strategy
Content is the core product. The app lives or dies by content quality, accuracy, and ADHD-friendly presentation.

5.1 Research Sources
	•	PubMed / NIH — peer-reviewed studies (label: Research-Backed)
	•	ACOG, Endocrine Society, APA — clinical guidelines (label: Expert Consensus)
	•	Cleveland Clinic Functional Medicine, IFM — functional approach (label: Functional Medicine)
	•	Peer-reviewed TCM journals — eastern medicine (label: Traditional / Eastern)
	•	University health centers (Harvard, Mayo, Johns Hopkins) — credible summaries

5.2 Content Creation Workflow
	•	Research topic using primary sources (PubMed, clinical guidelines)
	•	Summarize key findings into ADHD-friendly format (short, visual, action-oriented)
	•	Label evidence strength for every claim
	•	Add ADHD connection and pattern-spotting guidance
	•	Medical review — have a licensed practitioner review before publishing
	•	Publish to app via CMS (Airtable or Notion)

Risk: Medical Content Liability
Always include clear disclaimers: 'This is educational, not medical advice.' Never use diagnostic language. Have a licensed healthcare provider review all content before publishing. Consider consulting a health law attorney for your Terms of Service.

5.3 Content Volume for MVP Launch
	•	7 full topic cards (one per health area listed in Section 2.1)
	•	30 days of tracking data schema built and ready
	•	5–7 pattern insights pre-written (shown after 2+ weeks of tracking)


6. Monetization Model
6.1 Freemium Subscription
Free Tier
Premium ($9.99/mo or $79.99/yr)
	•	3 topic cards
	•	7-day symptom tracker
	•	Basic daily check-in
	•	Full library (all topics)
	•	Unlimited tracking + history
	•	Pattern insights + charts
	•	Doctor appointment export
	•	New content first access

Why freemium for ADHD users?
ADHD users need to experience value before they commit. Free access builds the habit loop. Premium conversion happens after they see their own patterns emerge — usually weeks 2–4. Annual plans reduce churn significantly for this audience.

8. Immediate Next Steps
Ordered by priority. Complete each step before moving to the next.

Your goal in the next 30 days
Validate that real users want this — before spending on development. This saves tens of thousands of dollars and confirms your concept.

Week 1–2: Content Validation
	•	Build 2–3 sample topic cards in Notion or Airtable in the exact ADHD-friendly format (Summary → ADHD Connection → Pattern Spotters → Action Steps)
	•	Share with 5–10 women in ADHD communities (Reddit: r/ADHDwomen, Facebook groups)
	•	Collect feedback: Is this format helpful? What's missing? Would you pay for this?

Week 2–3: Prototype
	•	Set up a Glide App or Airtable interface with your sample content
	•	Add a simple daily check-in form (Google Form or Airtable form)
	•	Invite 10–20 beta users to test for 2 weeks

Week 3–4: Legal & Business Foundation
	•	Register your business entity (LLC recommended)
	•	Consult a health law attorney on: medical disclaimer language, Terms of Service, privacy policy, HIPAA considerations for health tracking data
	•	Purchase your domain name

Week 4+: Build Decision
	•	Analyze beta feedback — is content format validated? Are users tracking daily?
	•	If yes: scope your dev budget and post for a React Native developer (Toptal, Upwork, or a dev agency)
	•	If no: iterate on content format and repeat validation
